What is an MOT Check?

MOT stands for Ministry of Transport, and the test is a mandatory annual assessment for vehicles over three years old in the UK. It evaluates the roadworthiness of a vehicle and ensures it meets the necessary safety and environmental standards.

During an MOT check, various components of your vehicle are inspected, including brakes, tires, lights, exhaust systems, and more. A successful MOT test means your vehicle is safe to drive, while a failure indicates issues that must be addressed before the vehicle can legally return to the road.

Why is an MOT Check Important?

  • Ensures Road Safety: Regular inspections reduce the risk of accidents by identifying potential mechanical issues.
  • Environmental Protection: Ensures vehicles meet emissions standards, minimizing pollution.
  • Legal Compliance: Driving without a valid MOT certificate can lead to fines, penalties, or even invalidation of your insurance.
  • Protects Resale Value: A vehicle with a clean MOT history is often more attractive to buyers.

What Does an MOT Check Cover?

  • Lighting and Signaling: Headlights, indicators, brake lights, and fog lights must all function correctly.
  • Brakes: The effectiveness and balance of the braking system are tested.
  • Tires and Wheels: Tires must have a minimum tread depth of 1.6mm and be free of damage.
  • Steering and Suspension: The steering mechanism must be responsive, and suspension components should be in good condition.
  • Exhaust and Emissions: The exhaust system must not leak, and emissions must meet legal standards.
  • Visibility: Windscreen, wipers, and washers must function properly, and mirrors must provide clear visibility.
  • Vehicle Structure: The body and chassis should be free of corrosion or damage.
  • Number Plates: Plates must be clean, legible, and comply with legal formatting.

How to Prepare for an MOT Check

  1. Check Lights: Ensure all exterior lights are working.
  2. Inspect Tires: Confirm proper tread depth and inflation.
  3. Clean the Vehicle: A dirty car can make it harder for the tester to see issues.
  4. Top Up Fluids: Refill windscreen washer fluid and ensure brake fluid is at the correct level.
  5. Fix Minor Issues: Replace blown bulbs, worn wiper blades, and check mirrors for damage.

What Happens if Your Vehicle Fails?

If your vehicle fails its MOT, you will be issued a “Refusal of an MOT Test Certificate,” which details the reasons for failure. These issues must be repaired before the vehicle can be retested.

Types of Failures:

  • Major Failures: These make the vehicle unsafe to drive until repairs are completed.
  • Dangerous Failures: These pose a direct threat to road safety or the environment. Driving the vehicle in this condition is illegal.

How to Check Your MOT Status

To check your MOT status, visit the DVSA website or MOTCheck.org.

  1. Enter the vehicle’s registration number.
  2. View the MOT expiry date and history.
  3. Access details of past failures, advisories, and mileages.

Costs Involved in an MOT Check

The maximum fee for an MOT test is:

  • Cars: £54.85
  • Motorcycles: £29.65
  • Vans (Class 7): £58.60

Prices may vary for additional services or repairs.

Conclusion

An MOT check is essential for maintaining the safety, reliability, and legal compliance of your vehicle. Regular inspections not only ensure road safety but also help identify and address potential issues early.
